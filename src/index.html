Some History

In 1867 families began worshiping in homes in the village. Later they broke ground on West Street for a church building in 1868.

In 1894, land was purchased and construction began on Main Street by generations of immigrants from Ireland, Italy and other European nations. The church building was dedicated in 1895.

In 1970 the church located at 100 North Main Street was destroyed by a fire. They started celebrating mass in the school located at 245 North Main Street near the corner of Sneden Place West.

In 1995 the new church was completed, the first mass was celebrated on September 30, 1995. Later that year on November 4, 1995, Cardinal John Oâ€™Conner, Archbishop of New York dedicated the church and altar.

Since then the church has become more diverse with parishioners from 29 countries, including the Caribbean, Central and South America, Mexico, as well as the Philippines and Nigeria.

Food Pantry

In 2020 on November 23 the SJSB Daily Bread Food Pantry opened its doors for the first time, offering a Thanksgiving dinner for over 200 families.
