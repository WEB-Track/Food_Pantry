Some History

In 1867 families began worshiping in homes in the village. Later they broke ground on West Street for a church building in 1868.

In 1894, land was purchased and construction began on Main Street by generations of immigrants from Ireland, Italy and other European nations. The church building was dedicated in 1895.

In 1970 the church located at 100 North Main Street was destroyed by a fire. They started celebrating mass in the school located at 245 North Main Street near the corner of Sneden Place West.

In 1995 the new church was completed, the first mass was celebrated on September 30, 1995. Later that year on November 4, 1995, Cardinal John Oâ€™Conner, Archbishop of New York dedicated the church and altar.

Since then the church has become more diverse with parishioners from 29 countries, including the Caribbean, Central and South America, Mexico, as well as the Philippines and Nigeria.

Food Pantry

In 2020 on November 23 the SJSB Daily Bread Food Pantry opened its doors for the first time, offering a Thanksgiving dinner for over 200 families.

Mission Statement

We parishioners of Saint Joseph-Saint Boniface recognizing that, God has bestowed upon us many blessings. The Daily Bread Ministry Food Pantry serves as a vehicle and instrument to share the great abundance that God has showered on us with those who are in need within our community. Food is shared with love and respect to those who come for assistance. It is our intention that they receive not only food for their physical hunger. We would also share generous portions of kindness and grace to feed their spiritual and emotional hungers. We believe the words of Jesus that this is one way we can respond directly to God's love for us.

Our Vision

We demonstrate the love of Jesus Christ by providing the loving, compassionate distribution of food for the sustenance of those who are hungry in our community. Our mission is to help those less fortunate by providing food, promote the value of nutrition, increase self-sufficiency and instill hope. We envision a community in which everyone has access to sufficient nutritious food. In doing so, Daily Bread Ministry Food Pantry helps to ensure that food relief has a more meaningful and enduring impact.
